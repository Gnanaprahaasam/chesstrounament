Dr. N. Mahalingam along with effectual administration of Dr.M. Manickam, Chairman, and the resourcefulness of Shri. M. Dr. Mahalingam College of Engineering and Technology (MCET), is a private autonomous Engineering College (Affiliated to Anna University, Chennai) started in 1998 under the auspices of NIA Trust, a charitable educational trust functioning under Sakthi Group. Nachimuthu Polytechnic College (NPTC), Vanavarayar Institute of Agriculture (VIA) and 6 Schools are also governed by the NIA trust. MCET holds 24 years of excellence in. enlightening technical education to young minds.

MCET has been accredited by NAAC with the highest A++ grade for the period 2018-2023 and MCET is the only engineering institution from Tamil Nadu to be awarded the highest NAAC grade consecutively in Cycle 1 & 2 MCET awards 2.5 crore rupees as scholarships to merit cum-mean students under Arutchelvar Vidhyasakthi Scholarship (AVS) and Dr. NM Tuition Fee Waiver Scheme (TFW)

Four Techno commercial ventures (VVDN, Caresoft, ABT Maruti, ABT infocloud) are functioning inside the campus with 18 Centers of Excellence for up skilling students' practical knowledge and an extra mile to industrial automation. A salient feature is the consistent milestone in placement with 90% every year, with 150+ prominent recruiters.

The Founder: Arutchelvar Dr. N. Mahalingam

      
         Arutchelvar  Dr. N Mahalingam was the Founder Chairman of Sakthi  Group,    6,000 crore industrial conglomerate operating in sugar, automobiles, finance, power  and logistics. As a polymath, he excelled in industry, entrepreneurship, culture, Gandhian politics, sports, religion, spirituality, philosophy, journalism etc. He received the Padma Bhushan award from the Government of India in 2007 for his contributions to the nation in the field of Trade and Industry.   He was born on 21st March, 1923 to    Thiru. P. Nachimuthu Gounder in an agriculture family at Pollachi. He was the     founder of many educational institutions, from schools to colleges.  Some of his educational institutions are Kumaraguru College of Technology, Nachimuthu Polytechnic College, Dr. Mahalingam College of Engineering & Technology, NGM Arts College, Kumaraguru Institute of Agriculture and Kumaraguru College of Liberal Arts and Science.

Dr. Mahalingam's contribution to chess in India and in particular Tamil Nadu is immense. As the president of All India Chess Federation and Tamil Nadu State Chess Association, he championed the growth and development of chess in the county and the state. It is worth mentioning to say that the five times world champion Vishy Anand achieved his final GM norm in the Sakthi Sugars GM tournament organised by Dr. Mahalingam at Coimbatore. He was also the President of Tamil Nadu Basketball Association for many number of years.

Arutchelvar Dr. N. Mahalingam was a chess philanthropist beyond comparison. His compassion to chess players in distress was phenomenal. He instituted incentives to players who achieved international and grand master titles which inspired the chess players to higher efforts.

General Information to Players

Entry Fee Structure

Ordinary entry: Rs. 1250/-(Rupees One Thousand Two Hundred Fifty Only)

Special Entry: Rs. 6250/-(Rupees Six Thousand Two Hundred Fifty Only)

There will be a 50% concession in the entry fee for the participants from north eastern states and Andaman & Nicobar islands.

Entry fee will be accepted strictly online through: https://prs.aict.in/entry. For clarification and guidance regarding submission of entries, please contact: aicfnationationalentries@gmail.com.

1. Late fee: Please note that the entry fee structure is valid till/th April 2022. Thereafter, a late fee of Rs. 250/-per player will be levied. No entry will be entertained after 11th April 2022 (upto 18:00 hrs). 

2. System of Play: The championship will be played under FIDE's Laws of Chess and the Swiss system.
Totally, eleven rounds shall be played Players from the same state shall not be paired against each other in the last round. However, they may be paired if there is no compatible opponent with the same point group or score group with a maximum difference of one paint.It is applicable only for the top 50% score of the penultimate round. 

3. Default Time: Default time for arriving to the chess board is 15 minutes.

4. Time Control: The rate of play shall be 90 minutes with an increment of 30 seconds per move from move.

number one.

5.Chess Clocks: Each participating State Chess Association must provide at least one clock in working order for

every two players entered into the championship. 13. Tie Break: Following is the order of tie-break:

(f) The greater number of wins by black In case of tie, cash prizes will not be shared.

6. Draw Agreement Players shall not agree for a draw without the consent of the arbiter, before each player makes 30 moves.

7. Protest: Protest, if any, against the decision of the Chief Arbiter shall be made in writing with a protest fee of Rs. 1000/-within 30 minutes of the occurrence of the incident. The protest fee will be refunded if the appealis upheld.

8. Appeals Committee: Before the commencement of the tournament, a five member appeals committee shall be formed by the AICF/organizers. All the members and reserves shall be from different states. No member of the Committee can vote on a dispute in which a player from his own state is involved and in such case(s) the reserve member(s) shall take his/her place in the committee.

All entries including special entries should come through the concerned state association. However, AICF will have the right to accept or reject any entry without assigning any reason. The entry form should be sent together with an attested copy of the original date of birth certificate (birth Certificates from Panchayat/Municipality/Corporation only will be accepted) 2019 Asian Under-16 Champion subject to age stipulation.

Eligibility 

a) Two players in each category from every affiliated State Association of AICF If any association fails to send its quota the vacancies so caused will not be filled.

b) Top four players from 2019 National Under 17 (Open & Girls) Chess Championships are eligible to participate

as seeded players in their respective categories subject to age stipulation.

c) The top two players from 2019 National Under 15 Open and Girls Championships are eligible to participate as seeded players in their respective categories.

d) The Champions from 2019 National Under 13 (Open & Girls) chess championships are eligible to participate as seeded players in their respective category.

e) World Champion in any over the board category from the year 2019 subject to age stipulation. 

f) 2019 World No. 2&3 in Under-16 and Under-18 categories subject to age stipulation.

g) 2019 Asian Under-18 top three rankers subject to age stipulation.

h) The winner and runner up of 2019 National Under-17 Girls Championship are eligible to play in the open section, subject to age stipulation.

i) The winner of 2019 National Under-15 Girls Championship is eligible to play in open section. Current over the board national champion in any higher category subject to age stipulation.

j) All GMs/IMs are seeded in the Open Section and all WGMS /WIMs are seeded in Girls Section subject to age stipulation.

k) Any other player who remits the prescribed special entry fee of Rs. 6,250/- Such entries should come through the respective affiliated State Association. 

l) Host Tamil Nadu State Chess Association is entitled to fill two additional players in each category.

m) The AICF Secretary has a discretionary seat in ch category in this Championship. 

n) Two players in each category approved by AICF will be permitted from areas where no State Association exists. 

o) If the number of participants at the start of the championship is an odd number in a category, the host association can add one entry to make it even

p) Only Indian nationals are eligible to participate in the championships.

q) In addition to the above, three additional entries from the state which won gold, two entries from the state which won the silver and one additional entry from the state which won the bronze in the Under-17 (Open & Girls) Chess Championships 2019 shall be accepted. Open: West Bengal (Gold, Silver & Bronze)-6. Girls: Orissa (Gold)-3, Delhi (Silver)-2 & Tamil Nadu (Bronze)-1. And these entries from the state associations shall be given only from the ranklist of their respective state championships with proof and not to anybody else.
Age Proof: Each participant must produce a valid certificate to prove his/her age.

Age Limit: Players should be born on or after 1.1.2004.

Certificates from School will not be accepted. Certificates should preferably be from the Panchayat, Municipality or  Corporation obtained soon after birth. Players without date of birth certificates will not be allowed to participate.

The date of birth certificates registered within one year of the birth of the player alone will be recognized and allowed to participate. No state association shall recommend any player who doesn't come under the above criteria.

However, player/state can appeal to AICF in this regard as per the notification published at https://aicf.in/public notice-dob-issue/with undertaking and supporting documents.

If any doubt arises about the claimed age of a player, the Tournament Committee has the right to refer the player during     the championship for a due evaluation of age by competent medical experts. A player if declared over-age by such experts shall not continue in the championship and the matter shall be reported to AICF. Protest about the age of players may be made to the Chief Arbiter in writing along with a fee of Rs.5000/- per player. This will not be refunded. No protest about the over age of a player will be entertained after the second round of the championship.

Only the Coach or Manager deputed by the participating State Chess Association can lodge such protests. The Tournament Committee has the right to decide the day, time and place for such medical evaluation of age. AICF/Organizers will have the right to refer any player to the medical evaluation of age at any time during the Championship. Players refusing to take the test as decided by the Tournament Committee shall be deemed to have failed the test and shall be disqualified from the Championship.

Dress Code

As per the AICF regulations on dress code for players participating in all the National championships, all state associations should send their players including special/donor entry players with proper uniform (as regards special/doner entry players, the respective state chess associations may collect the cost of the uniform from the players). Players should at least wear uniform     T-Shirts/formal shirts (same colour and brand with the name of the State printed prominently at the back and the state chess emblem in the front left side). The name/logo of the sponsors can be permitted. Girls/Women have also to be given appropriate uniform. The above rule will also be applicable to special units Players without properuniform will not be permitted to take part in the championship. This will be strictly enforced.
